Research

“Regulation and Market Power: Evidence from the Manufactured Housing Industry”

In this paper, I study the mortgage market for manufactured homes (also known as mobile homes) using loan-level Home Mortgage Disclosure Act data. I first document how post-crisis regulations like stress testing worsened market power issues in this market. Regulation raised costs for large banks relative to other lenders. Their total market share subsequently declined from about 25% to less than 5%, and prices increased for borrowers. Meanwhile, the largest nonbank firm in the market grew to have nearly a 40% national market share. I then study a second regulation that improved consumer welfare. In 2014 an interest rate restriction on the highest priced loans lowered interest rates with minimal impact on supply, increasing consumer welfare. I estimate consumer welfare using a logit demand model and will estimate counterfactual welfare under different interest rate restrictions.
